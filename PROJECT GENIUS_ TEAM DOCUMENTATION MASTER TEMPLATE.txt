3. PRODUCT JOURNEY
Phase A: Ground Potentializing (Research & Ideation)
The team observed a stark contrast in Uyo: communities self-organize around visible problems like overflowing waste bins or failed water supply, but they ignore air pollution because it is invisible. There was no "shared signal" to coordinate action. The team initially considered a traditional "machine-like" system (deploy sensors -> report data), but realized these systems are fragile—they degrade silently when funding or attention fades.
* The Problem: Air pollution's delayed and abstract health effects impede effective action. Residents struggle to prioritize long-term risks, and policymakers lack the public urgency and definitive data needed for stringent regulations. The slow onset of severe health issues obscures the link between emission sources and impact, hindering a coordinated public and political response.
* Key Research Insight: "Communities act when information flows through them, not at them... We shifted from a rigid machine to a Living System."
* https://drive.google.com/open?id=1eVZSkCLen2Bk7ukteQ8gOhAzRKFGhB3N 






Phase B: Prototyping (Design & Development)
To build a "Living System," AirChain designed sensors as "Cells"—autonomous nodes that tolerate intermittent power and connectivity loss. Crucially, they implemented Merkle Batching for data integrity. Instead of spamming the blockchain with raw data, the system hashes readings into a Merkle tree and anchors only the Root on Cardano. This ensures privacy, efficiency, and verifiable truth without bloating the chain.
* Top 3 Features:
   * Resilient Nodes: Sensors that buffer data during outages and sync when the network recovers.
   * Merkle Data Anchoring: Efficiently proving the existence of vast amounts of environmental data using a single on-chain hash.
   * AIRQ Token: A pre-minted token (Supply: 1B) designed to incentivize stewardship and maintenance of the physical hardware.
Phase C: Deployment (Final Implementation)
The system is fully operational on the Cardano Preprod Testnet. The backend uses FastAPI and Kubernetes for orchestration, ensuring scalability. An Aiken Validator enforces the integrity of the data anchors, treating Cardano as "immutable public memory" rather than a computation engine.
* Technical Stack: FastAPI (Python), Kubernetes, MQTT (Ingestion), Cardano (Aiken/Plutus v3), PostgreSQL.
* Live Status: Operational; Validator deployed on Preprod; AIRQ Tokens minted.
* https://drive.google.com/open?id=1vIZBaI_7MMTugHTFzhg0J0mNIxuAK9Mn

3. PRODUCT JOURNEY
Phase A: Ground Potentializing (Research & Ideation)
The Tinkers team identified a critical vulnerability in the current healthcare system: medical records are often stored on paper or in centralized databases that are prone to loss, alteration, or compromise. The research highlighted that when a patient’s history is tampered with, it isn't just a data breach—it is a direct threat to patient safety. The team realized that trust in medical data should not depend on a single administrator but on an immutable standard of truth.
* The Problem: Centralized, paper medical records pose significant security risks from easy tampering (lack of audit trail enables fraud) and high loss vulnerability (fire, flood, misfiling, fragmentation). This necessitates adopting secure, distributed, and immutable digital solutions.
* Key Research Insight: "The risk goes beyond data; it is a threat to patient safety."
* https://drive.google.com/open?id=1EBMd_eg87VijbqiX69dGV-2absgG_KD3 






Phase B: Prototyping (Design & Development)
To solve this, the team built T-Medic, a platform that functions as a "Digital Notary." The prototype adopts a hybrid approach: patient records are stored securely in a standard database for accessibility, but a unique "digital fingerprint" (hash) of that record is generated for verification. The team chose Cardano (a 3rd-generation, peer-reviewed blockchain) for its native metadata capabilities, allowing them to anchor these proofs without high fees or excessive energy consumption.
* Top 3 Features:
   * Hybrid Storage: Secure off-chain database for privacy, on-chain anchoring for truth.
   * "Certify on Cardano": One-click wallet signature to mint record proofs.
   * Tamper Flagging: Immediate invalidation if the current record does not match the blockchain fingerprint.
Phase C: Deployment (Final Implementation)
The transparent, immutable certification process ensures a fully auditable and tamper-proof record. It begins with data entry to create an accurate record. The user then selects "Certify," triggering a swift blockchain transaction. This transaction embeds a unique identifier for the certified record within its metadata, acting as a cryptographic fingerprint. A small ADA fee is required to submit and permanently anchor the record to the decentralized Cardano ledger, making it irreversible. Finally, a verification link is generated, allowing anyone—auditors, regulators, or the public—to instantly audit the record on Cardanoscan. This public audit confirms the record's integrity by comparing the unique identifier, proving it hasn't been altered since certification.
* Technical Stack: Fullstack Web, Cardano Blockchain (Metadata/Transaction), Wallet Integration.
* Live Status: Functional MVP on Cardano Preprod Testnet.
* https://drive.google.com/open?id=10ZAG4Tz1bt0FRxZj6XQBlgvTEWB84C0F

The team’s journey began in Zaria (Samaru Market) and Kaduna (Abidjan Dakar/Bamako Street). Their initial assumption was that the community's biggest struggles were electricity and water. However, by observing overflowing dumpsites and blocked drains, they realized the deeper issue was waste mismanagement affecting soil fertility and food security. During the build phase, the team faced a major dilemma: How to separate plastic from organic waste? They initially considered high-tech solutions like AI-sensitive cameras and robotic arms. However, realizing that limited resources and time would make this unsustainable for the community, they pivoted. They designed a low-tech, high-impact "2-in-1" bin.
* Top 3 Features:
   * Propeller Compositing Bin: A mechanical bin where propellers serve as both a sieve to separate plastics and a turning tool for compost.
   * Pyrolysis Unit: A system that heats the separated plastics (in the absence of oxygen) to convert them into synthetic fuel.

The final solution is a "GreenToken Ledger." It combines the physical processing of waste with a digital record book on Cardano. * Key Research Insight: If a student owns their data via a Decentralized Identifier (DID), they become the custodian of their own future, not the school.

3. PRODUCT JOURNEY
Phase A: Ground Potentializing (Research & Ideation)
The team immersed themselves in Ashaka, Gombe State, identifying a chronic crisis where electricity is available for only 1-2 hours at night. This crippled the livelihoods of artisans like Mallam Abdullaziz, whose machinery sat idle during the day. The team found that vandalism (costing the community ₦2 million in repairs) and a lack of trust in the grid were major blockers.
* The Problem: Unreliable power, exacerbated by infrastructure vandalism, is severely eroding community trust and economic productivity. Frequent, unscheduled outages disrupt life and essential services. Theft and destruction of utility components destabilize the grid, impose financial burdens, and breed cynicism. This instability increases business operating costs, causes production losses, stifles growth, deters investment, and diminishes national output.
* Key Research Insight: "Value means reliable visibility... shifting from 23% uptime planning to coordinated micro-grid use."

Phase B: Prototyping (Design & Development)
The team initially planned for centralized alerts but pivoted after resident input showed a need for community ownership. They built the Ashaka Energy Commons, a mobile-first webapp that turns residents into "Energy Stewards."
* Input: Residents log ON/OFF events and report risks ("Problem spotted") via the webapp.
* Process: Local validators confirm the reports.
* Reward: The system mints Energy Steward Tokens to reward active monitoring, creating a localized "immune system" against vandalism.
* Top 3 Features:

   * Stewardship Rewards: Token incentives for validating grid status, gamifying maintenance.
   * Visibility Dashboard: Real-time data for local leaders to coordinate usage and protect assets.
The final solution anchors these community reports on the Cardano blockchain. This ensures that the data regarding outages and repairs is tamper-proof, eliminating middlemen and building a trusted history of the community's energy profile.
