## ðŸ“Š Judging Criteria

Prototypes are evaluated on four key criteria:

### 1. Clarity of Problem (25%)

**What judges look for:**
- Is the problem clearly defined?
- Do you know who it affects and how?
- Is it backed by field research evidence?
- Can you articulate the root cause?

**Good example:**
> "200+ Sabon Gari vendors lose 30-40% revenue during rains due to blocked drains from plastic waste. Individual collection costs â‚¦200/day (too expensive). Previous collective system failed due to payment coordination."

**Poor example:**
> "Waste is a problem in markets."

---

### 2. Prototype Relevance (30%)

**What judges look for:**
- Does the prototype address the root cause?
- Is it appropriate for the context (tech level, cost, maintenance)?
- Does it build on existing practices?
- Is it locally maintainable?

**Good example:**
> "Shared collection drum with SMS payment (vendors already use mobile money). Simple mechanical design (local metal workers can repair). Builds on existing recycling cooperative relationship."

**Poor example:**
> "Complex IoT sensor system requiring cloud infrastructure and smartphone app (vendors have basic phones)."

---

### 3. Evidence of Testing (30%)

**What judges look for:**
- Have you tested with real users?
- Do you have quotes/feedback from testing?
- Photos/video of people using the prototype?
- What did you learn from testing?

**Good example:**
> "Tested payment willingness with 15 vendors: 13/15 (87%) said yes at â‚¦50/day. Tested drum placement with market manager + 5 vendors. Manager approved location; vendors confirmed it's on their daily path."

**Poor example:**
> "We plan to test it next week" or "Our friends said it's a good idea."

---

### 4. Potential to Scale (15%)

**What judges look for:**
- Clear next steps for expansion
- Realistic resource requirements
- Path to sustainability
- Evidence of demand

**Good example:**
> "Pilot with 1 drum in Block A (200 vendors). If successful after 2 weeks, expand to Blocks B-D (600 more vendors). Market manager committed to official support if pilot works. Recycling cooperative confirmed they'll buy sorted plastic."

**Poor example:**
> "We'll scale to all markets in Nigeria" (with no evidence or plan).
