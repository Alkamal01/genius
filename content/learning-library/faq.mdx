**A:** Follow these steps:

## ğŸ¯ Project Scope & Strategy

### Q: Our idea is too big. How do we scope it down?

**A:** Great question! Follow the "Smallest Testable Version" approach:

**Instead of:** "An app for all market vendors in the city"
**Start with:** "SMS system for 5 onion vendors in one section"

**Ask yourself:**
- What's the **core problem** we're solving?
- What's the **smallest version** that tests our assumption?
- Can we test it with **5 people in 3 days**?

**Rule of thumb:** If you can't test it with real users by Dec 06, it's too big.

## ğŸ“± Communication & Updates

### Q: How often should we update our dev log?

**A:** **Every day you work on the project**, even if it's just 30 minutes.

**Good dev log entries include:**
- What you did today (be specific)
- What you learned or discovered
- What you're doing next
- Any blockers or questions

**Example:**
```
## Dec 04, 2024
- Interviewed 3 vendors at Sabon Gari market
- Learned: They already use WhatsApp for coordination
- Next: Design SMS system that integrates with existing WhatsApp groups
- Blocker: Need to understand mobile money API
```

### Q: Can we change our project idea after starting?

**A:** Yes, **but do it early** (before Dec 06 if possible).

**Good reasons to pivot:**
- User research revealed a different, more urgent problem
- Your original idea isn't technically feasible in the timeframe
- You discovered someone already solved your problem well

**Bad reasons to pivot:**
- It's harder than you thought (push through!)
- You saw another team's "cooler" idea
- You haven't tested with users yet (test first!)

**Process:**
1. Talk to a mentor first
2. Update your PRD
3. Notify the Hub team
4. Document why you pivoted (this is valuable learning!)

### Q: Do we need a working prototype?

**A:** You need **evidence of testing**, not necessarily a fully working product.

**Acceptable evidence:**
- Paper prototype tested with 5 users + their feedback
- Working SMS system tested with 3 vendors + quotes
- Physical prototype (e.g., collection drum) + photos of people using it
- Wizard-of-Oz test (you manually do what the tech would do) + documentation

**Not acceptable:**
- "We plan to test it next week"
- "Our friends said it's a good idea"
- "We built it but haven't shown it to anyone yet"

**Key principle:** Real feedback from real users in real contexts.
