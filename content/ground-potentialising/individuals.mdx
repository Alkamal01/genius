## ðŸ’¡ Tips for Quality Sensing Entries

### Writing Good Headlines

âœ… **Specific and actionable:**
- "Sabon Gari vendors lack affordable collective waste collection"
- "ABU students miss classes due to unreliable campus shuttle timing"

âŒ **Too vague:**
- "Market has problems"
- "Transportation issues"

### Capturing Good Quotes

âœ… **Direct, specific, attributed:**
- Record exact words, not your interpretation
- Include speaker's name (or "Anonymous") and role
- Provide context for when/why they said it

âŒ **Poor quotes:**
- Paraphrased: "She said the market was dirty"
- No attribution: "Someone told me..."
- No context: Just the quote with no explanation

### Taking Effective Photos

âœ… **Contextual and clear:**
- Show the problem in its environment
- Include people (with consent) to show scale and impact
- Caption explains what we're seeing and why it matters

âŒ **Poor photos:**
- Too close up, no context
- Blurry or poorly lit
- No caption or vague caption

### Writing Reflections

âœ… **Honest and specific:**
- What surprised you (assumptions that were wrong)
- Specific numbers, names, details
- What you still don't understand
- Concrete next steps

âŒ **Generic reflections:**
- "It was interesting"
- "The problem is bad"
- No specific learnings or next steps

### Insufficient Evidence
- âŒ Only 1-2 quotes instead of 3+

### Vague Observations
- âŒ "People are unhappy about waste"

- âŒ Just listing facts without analysis

## ðŸ“Š Quality Standards

Your sensing entry will be evaluated on:

### Evidence Quality (40%)
- Are quotes direct and attributed?
- Do photos show the problem clearly?
- Is video/audio included and transcribed?
- Is consent properly documented?

### Insight Depth (30%)
- Did you talk to multiple people?
- Do you understand root causes, not just symptoms?
- Are your observations specific and detailed?
- Did you identify patterns or surprises?

### Reflection Quality (20%)
- Is your reflection honest about what you don't know?
- Do you articulate why this matters?
- Are next steps specific and actionable?
- Did you challenge your own assumptions?

### Ethical Practice (10%)
- Was consent obtained properly?
- Did you respect people's time and privacy?
- Are people represented fairly and accurately?
- Did you offer reciprocity (sharing findings, offering help)?
