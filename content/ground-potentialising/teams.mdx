## Preliminary Solution Idea / Prototype Description

### Solution Overview
A shared waste collection drum with SMS-based micro-payment system. Vendors send â‚¦50 via mobile money when they deposit waste. Collector is paid automatically when drum is full and emptied. Visual fill-level marker shows community progress.

### How It Works
1. **Collection drum** installed at market entrance (one per block initially)
2. **Vendors deposit waste** and send â‚¦50 via mobile money (simple SMS code)
3. **Visual marker** shows drum fill level (painted graduations)
4. **Collector empties drum** when full, receives automatic payment from pooled funds
5. **Recycling cooperative** buys sorted plastic from collector

### Why This Approach
- **Builds on existing:** Vendors already use mobile money; collector already has recycling buyer
- **Solves coordination:** Automated payment removes collection burden
- **Visible progress:** Fill marker creates social proof and collective accountability
- **Locally maintainable:** Simple mechanical drum, no complex tech to break
- **Regenerative:** Creates reliable income for collector, reduces vendor costs, cleaner market

## ðŸ’¡ Tips for Quality Team Discovery Logs

### Synthesizing Individual Research

âœ… **Good synthesis:**
- Combines findings from multiple team members
- Identifies patterns across interviews
- Shows progression from individual observations to team insights

âŒ **Poor synthesis:**
- Just lists individual findings without connection
- No analysis of patterns or contradictions
- Doesn't show how team thinking evolved

### Writing Strong Problem Statements

âœ… **Specific and evidence-backed:**
- Names specific people/groups affected
- Quantifies impact where possible
- Explains root cause, not just symptoms
- Backed by quotes and data from research

âŒ **Vague problem statements:**
- "People have waste problems"
- No specific location or people
- No evidence of impact
- Confuses symptoms with root causes

### Describing Solutions

âœ… **Clear and testable:**
- Explains how it works in simple steps
- Shows why this addresses root cause
- Identifies what's needed to build/test it
- Acknowledges what you don't know yet

âŒ **Unclear solutions:**
- Vague "we'll build an app"
- No explanation of how it solves the problem
- No consideration of feasibility
- Overconfident without testing

## ðŸ“Š Quality Standards

Your team discovery log will be evaluated on:

### Research Quality (30%)
- Multiple perspectives (vendors, collectors, managers, etc.)
- Sufficient sample size for claims
- Proper documentation of evidence
- Ethical consent practices

### Problem Understanding (25%)
- Clear articulation of who, what, where, why
- Root cause analysis, not just symptoms
- Quantified impact where possible
- Evidence-backed claims

### Solution Design (25%)
- Addresses root cause identified in research
- Builds on existing practices/systems
- Feasible with available resources
- Clear testing plan

### Team Collaboration (20%)
- Evidence of multiple team members contributing
- Synthesis of individual findings
- Clear roles and next steps
- Honest about blockers and needs

## ðŸ”„ Updating Your Discovery Log

### When to Update

- **After each field visit:** Add new evidence, quotes, photos
- **After team meetings:** Update problem statement, solution ideas
- **After testing:** Document results and learnings
- **Weekly minimum:** Keep "Next Steps" and "Last Updated" current

### What to Update

- Evidence list (as you collect more)
- Research notes (new interviews, findings)
- Problem statement (as understanding deepens)
- Solution description (as you iterate)
- Tests done (document everything you try)
- Next steps (always keep current)

## ðŸš« Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Insufficient Evidence
- âŒ Problem statement not backed by research
- âŒ Only talked to 1-2 people
- âŒ No photos or quotes

**Fix:** Do more field research before finalizing problem statement

### Solution Before Problem
- âŒ Started with a technology and looked for a problem
- âŒ Didn't validate problem with users first
- âŒ Solution doesn't address root cause

**Fix:** Go back to field research, understand the problem deeply first

### No Testing
- âŒ Built solution without user feedback
- âŒ Assumptions not validated
- âŒ No evidence solution would work

**Fix:** Test early and often, even with paper prototypes

### Poor Team Coordination
- âŒ One person doing all the work
- âŒ Duplicate research efforts
- âŒ No clear roles or next steps

**Fix:** Regular team meetings, clear task assignments, shared documentation
