## The Five Dimensions of Regenerative Design

### 1. Customers/Beneficiaries: Beyond Surface Needs
Not just "What do people want?" but "What are the deep aspirations that animate their lives?" Not just "transactions" but "transformation."
*   *Example:* A marketplace isn't just about buying vegetables‚Äîit's about dignity, social connection, economic agency, and the pride of providing for family.

### 2. Co-creators: The Team as Living System
Recognition that the Weaver, Scribe, Developers, and Bridge aren't just roles but capacities that must be nurtured. How is the team itself becoming more regenerative through this work?

### 3. Earth: The Silent Stakeholder
The planet and ecological health aren't externalities‚Äîthey're primary stakeholders. Does this solution extract or regenerate natural capital? Does it increase or decrease environmental health?
*   *Question:* "Would the land itself approve of what we're building?"

### 4. Community: The Social Fabric
Beyond the direct users, how does this affect the entire web of relationships in places like Samaru, Ashaka, and Uyo? Does it strengthen trust networks or weaken them? Does it honor or erode cultural patterns?

### 5. Investors: Capital as Regenerative Energy
Reframing funding not as extraction-seeking missiles but as patient capital seeking long-term systemic health. What returns matter beyond financial ones?

## The Transformation
Each team mapped their solution across all five dimensions, discovering where they were strong and where they'd been blind. Many realized they'd been designing for dimension #1 while ignoring dimensions #3, #4, and #5 entirely.

Apps became ecosystems. Features became relationships. Code became containers for regenerative possibility.
