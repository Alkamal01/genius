## ğŸ“Š Judging Criteria

### 1. Completeness of Submission (15%)

**What judges look for:**
- All required files present
- Proper file naming and organization
- Clear, well-written documentation
- Professional presentation

**Scoring:**
- 100%: Everything present, well-organized, professional
- 75%: Minor items missing or poorly organized
- 50%: Significant gaps in documentation
- 25%: Minimal documentation, hard to understand

---

### 2. Quality of Documentation (15%)

**What judges look for:**
- Clear problem statement
- Detailed research process
- Evidence of iteration
- Honest reflection on failures
- Well-captioned photos and videos

**Good example:**
\u003e Complete journey documented from initial research through multiple iterations. Clear before/after comparisons. Honest about what didn't work. Evidence shows evolution of thinking.

**Poor example:**
\u003e Sparse documentation. Only shows final version. No evidence of process or iteration.

---

### 3. Evidence of Real-World Impact (25%)

**What judges look for:**
- Deployed solution (not just prototype)
- Real users actively using it
- Measurable improvements
- Testimonials from beneficiaries
- Documented behavior change

**Strong evidence:**
- \"50 vendors now using the system daily. Average waste collection increased from 0 to 30kg/day. Vendors report 20% reduction in cleanup time. Market manager confirmed drain blockages reduced by 60%.\"

**Weak evidence:**
- \"People said they liked it\" or \"We think it will help\"

---

### 4. Clarity of Problem (10%)

**What judges look for:**
- Clear articulation of who, what, where, why
- Root cause identified
- Evidence-backed claims
- Specific numbers and details

---

### 5. Prototype Relevance (10%)

**What judges look for:**
- Addresses root cause
- Appropriate for context
- Builds on existing practices
- Locally maintainable
- Evidence of user acceptance

---

### 6. Evidence of Testing (10%)

**What judges look for:**
- Multiple test iterations
- Real user feedback incorporated
- Documented learnings
- Honest about failures
- Clear improvement over time

---

### 7. Potential to Scale (15%)

**What judges look for:**
- Clear path to expansion
- Realistic resource requirements
- Partnership commitments
- Revenue/sustainability model
- Evidence of demand beyond pilot

**Strong plan:**
\u003e \"Pilot with 200 vendors successful. Market manager committed to official support. 3 other markets requested same system. Recycling cooperative confirmed capacity to handle 5x current volume. Need \u20a6200,000 for 5 additional drums and SMS system expansion.\"

**Weak plan:**
\u003e \"We'll expand to all of Nigeria\" (no evidence or realistic plan)
